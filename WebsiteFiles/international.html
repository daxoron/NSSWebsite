International Girl Child Day

Shyama Prasad Mukherji College, Delhi University

he International Day of the Girl Child is being celebrated every year on October 11. The main objective of this day is to empower women and help them to get their rights so that they can face the challenges all over the world and meet their needs.NSS UNIT OF SPMC has organised different events and competitions to raise awareness about the challenges faced by girls across the world.

On the occasion of international girl child day, we have organised different events like:

  1. • JHANKAR (Traditional dance Competition)
  2. • EK JANG AB KHUD SE (Mono Acting Competition)
  3. • PRATEEK (Logo Making Competition)
  4. • PRADARSHAN (Video Making Competition)

JHANKAR


Jhankar is the one of the competition hosted by our NSS team which took place on October 11. All the participants was strickly instructed to send their entries through whatsapp only to Coordinators of the event i.e Prerna Khokhar and saachi.There are some rules for participants:- Only solo performance was acceptable,Time limit for the same was 1minutes 30 seconds , Video exceeding this limit were disqualified, Only Traditional Dance forms were allowed.

Jhankar (Traditional dance Competition) went very smoothly , Students from different College participated very Enthusastically. It was proudly said that Nisha from Shyama Prasad Mukherji College was the Winner of Jhankar and she was awarded with Winning Certificate. All performance were plausible.


EK JANG AB KHUD SE

Ek Jang Ab Khud Se is the one of the competition hosted by our NSS team which took place on October 11. All the participants was strickly instructed to send their entries through whatsapp only to Coordinators of the event i.e Prerna Khokhar and Krishna Gupta. There are some rules for participants:- Time Limit was 4 minutes , video exceeding the limit were disqualified and plagiarism was strict prohibited. The theme for this competition was Girl child and feminism and the participants were allowed to use only 2 props.

This event was well coordinated. All the contestants participated very enthusatically. This competition was held smoothly. All the performances was plausible and their confidence was awesome. It was proudly said that Yashvi Agarwal from Shyama Prasad Mukherji College for Women was the winner of Mono Acting Competition and she was awarded with winning Certificates.

PRATEEK


Prateek is the one of the competition hosted by our NSS team which took place on October 11. All the participants was strickly instructed to send their entries through whatsapp only to Coordinators of the event i.e Prerna Khokhar , Bhawna Solanki and Shreya Pandey. There are some rules for participants:-The logo had to be presented in A4 size with white background and dimension for logo was 15cm × 15cm. Techniques could be anything like drawing, painting or computer design. The topic for the logo making competition was Women empowerment. plagiarism was strict prohibited. Mohammad Sibtain Kadri from Bharathidasan University was the winner of Logo Making competition and he was awarded with winning certificate. All the entries were awesome and I have no words for their creativity. Their designed logos were brilliant. We appreciated their work.

PRADARSHAN

PRADARSHAN (Video Making Competition) is the one of the competition hosted by our NSS team which took place on October 11. All the participants was strickly instructed to send their entries through whatsapp only to Coordinators of the event i.e Prerna Khokhar and shruti Borkataki . The time limit was of 1 minute and any kind of plagiarism was strictly prohibited. the tag line for which was "be bold to stop violence ,show your concern for the girl child.

PRADARSHAN (Video Making Competition) went very smoothly , Students from different College participated very Enthusastically. It was proudly said that the winner of Pradarshan was Rima from shyama Prasad Mukherji college and she was awarded with winning certificate. . All the entries were awesome and we have no words for their creativity and we appreciated their work.
